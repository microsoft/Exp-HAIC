Task Overview

In this stage, you will be presented with 20 different tasks. Each task consists of identifying which conditions are present in the X-ray of a dog (the "patient"). You will complete these tasks using a platform that we have designed for this study. Here is a screenshot of the interface on the platform.

The web page consists of of three main components. We'll now review each one of them in turn.

Interacting with the X-ray

For each of the cases, you will be shown a single X-ray of the patient. The X-ray is presented in the left hand side of the web pages. You will be able to interact with the image by
  • changing its brightness and contrast.
  • zooming in by double clicking on the region of interest.
  • dragging it around.
  • resetting it to its initial state.
  • making it full screen.

Making your diagnosis

In the column in the middle of the web pages, you will find a series of panels stacked on the top of each other. Each panel contains a set of three questions relative to each of 33 conditions. You will be asked to indicate:
  • The likelihood that the condition is present in the X-ray, on a scale from 0% to 100%.
  • Whether you believe that the condition is present in the X-ray (yes or no). A blue checkmark will appear in the navigation bar on the right hand side of the web page next to all conditions for which you selected "Yes".
  • Whether you would seek a second opinion from another colleague on the assessment (yes or no).

For each of the listed conditions, the default values of the questions above are set to "0%", "no", and "no" respectively. Thus, you should only focus on the conditions you suspect may be present in the X-ray.

We are particularly interested in your likelihood estimates for cases you are uncertain about. For example, in case of images of poor quality, we expect your and your colleagues' diagnoses to agree in terms of the likelihood estimates (e.g., estimated probabilities of the condition being present all around 50%), but likely not in terms of the binary predictions (condition is present vs. not present).

Interacting with the navigation bar

To help you navigate through the conditions more quickly, we have created a navigation bar. This bar, which is available on the right hand side of the web page, contains the 33 conditions grouped by macro categories. To navigate to a certain condition, you can first click on the category and then on the individual condition that will appear. See the GIF below for an example.
